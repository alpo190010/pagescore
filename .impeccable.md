# Design Context — PageScore

## Users
Shopify store owners, e-commerce sellers, DTC brand operators. Age 25-45, mobile-first, scan-not-read. Busy merchants who care about revenue, not design theory. They're not developers or designers — they want to sell more product. They arrive skeptical ("another tool"), leave convinced ("this one actually knows my page").

**Context of use:** Mid-workday, between Shopify admin tabs. Often on a phone or laptop. Short attention span, high intent. They paste a URL and want answers in seconds, not minutes.

**Job to be done:** "Show me what's costing me money and tell me exactly how to fix it."

## Brand Personality
- **Confident** — like a trusted advisor who's seen 10,000 product pages, not a salesperson
- **Sharp** — cuts through noise, says exactly what matters, never vague
- **Premium but accessible** — looks like it costs money, works like it's free
- **Analytical** — data-driven, specific, revenue-denominated ("$280/mo" not "could be better")
- **2026 modern** — not 2020 SaaS template, not 2024 dark mode everything

**Emotional arc:** Trust → Surprise → Urgency → Action. The user should feel "this tool gets me" first, then "I need to fix this NOW."

**Primary emotional goal:** Urgency + clarity. They see the problem and feel compelled to act immediately.

## Aesthetic Direction

### Visual Tone
Clean, warm, analytical. Generous whitespace with tight content blocks. Precision without coldness. Think: a beautifully typeset financial report, not a dashboard.

### References
- **Stripe** — premium feel, clarity of information hierarchy, confident use of space
- **Linear** — speed, polish, keyboard-first engineering aesthetic
- **Bloomberg terminal** — data density done right, monospace numbers that mean business
- **Arc Browser** — warm, thoughtful, opinionated. Design that has personality and point of view
- **Notion** — approachable sophistication. Makes complex things feel simple and inviting

### Anti-References (What This Must NOT Look Like)
- **Generic SaaS template** — no purple gradients, no floating blobs, no "Powered by AI" badges, no hero illustrations of people high-fiving. The 2020-2024 startup look is banned.
- **Cheap SEO tool** — no dashboard overload, no anxiety-inducing red warnings everywhere, no cluttered results pages, no 47 metrics crammed into one screen. No spinning score animations that feel like a slot machine.
- **Enterprise software** — no gray-on-gray, no sidebar navigation to nowhere, no "Contact Sales" energy.

### Color System
- **Background:** Warm off-white `#F8F7F4` — NOT clinical white
- **Surface:** Pure white `#FFFFFF` for cards
- **Primary accent:** Electric blue `#2563EB` — used sparingly for CTAs and focus
- **Semantic:** Red `#DC2626` (revenue loss, critical), Amber `#D97706` (warning), Green `#16A34A` (success, fixed)
- **Text:** Primary `#111111`, Secondary `#6B6B6B`, Tertiary `#9E9E9E`
- **Borders:** Subtle `#E5E7EB` — structure without weight

### Typography
- **Body:** Inter — clean, professional, excellent readability at all sizes
- **Data/Scores:** JetBrains Mono — monospace numbers that convey precision and authority
- **Hierarchy:** Size + weight only. No color tricks for hierarchy. Headlines are bold, tight tracking (`-0.02em`). Body is relaxed, generous line-height.

### Spacing & Layout
- Cards with subtle borders (`1.5px`), no heavy shadows
- Generous whitespace between sections, tight spacing within content blocks
- Max content width: `max-w-3xl` for text, `max-w-4xl` for cards/grids, `max-w-6xl` for full layouts
- Responsive: 390px mobile to 1440px desktop. Mobile-first, never an afterthought.

### Motion & Animation
- CSS animations preferred over JS libraries — keep bundle light
- Purposeful motion: score count-up, staggered card reveals, subtle fade-ups
- `prefers-reduced-motion` always respected
- Timing: ease-out for entrances, 200-400ms for UI transitions, 1200ms for the score reveal moment
- No bounces, no wobbles, no gratuitous particle effects

## What Makes It Memorable
The **score reveal moment** — number counts up in JetBrains Mono, revenue impact hits, user feels "I need to fix this." The diagnostic loader that tells you exactly what it's checking. The leak cards that are so specific the user thinks "how does it know my page?"

## Design Principles

1. **Revenue is the language** — Every metric, every card, every recommendation ties back to dollars lost or gained. Abstract scores are meaningless without financial context.

2. **Specificity builds trust** — Generic advice ("improve your images") is worthless. Specific advice ("Your hero image is 3.2MB and loads in 4.8s — costing ~$280/mo") is worth paying for.

3. **Progressive disclosure, not information dump** — Score first (1 number), then revenue impact (emotional hook), then categories (the breakdown), then fixes (the path forward). Never show everything at once.

4. **Warm precision** — Data-dense but never cold. Every number has context. Every warning has a solution. The interface feels like a knowledgeable friend, not a robot auditor.

5. **Earn the upsell** — The free scan must be genuinely useful on its own. The paid report is for people who want *more*, not people who got *nothing*.

## Quality Bar
Flagship — this is the entire product. Every pixel matters. No placeholder states. No "good enough" copy. No unstyled error messages. If a user screenshots this and shares it, it should look intentional.

## Technical Constraints
- Next.js 16 App Router
- Tailwind CSS 4
- No heavy JS animation libraries — CSS animations + `requestAnimationFrame` only
- Performance: fast initial load, no layout shift, instant input response
- Accessibility: WCAG AA minimum, focus-visible rings, screen reader labels, reduced motion support
- PostHog for analytics (already integrated)
